Implications for Ordination

The near-zero NMDS stress values for trees, herbs, and regeneration likely arise from matrix properties that ease low-dimensional rank fitting:

  • High tied dissimilarities: Many plot pairs have identical or near-identical species composition due to sparse matrices.
  • Sparse compositional structure: Low matrix fill percentages (4.9–15.8%) result in many zero dissimilarities.
  • Zero inflation: Particularly pronounced in herbs and regeneration strata.

These properties were considered when interpreting ordination results, and NMDS patterns were cross-checked against PERMANOVA, dispersion tests, and constrained ordination.
